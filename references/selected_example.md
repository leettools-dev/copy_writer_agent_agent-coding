Title: 2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report
Source: Anthropic (resources.anthropic.com)
URL: https://resources.anthropic.com/hubfs/2026%20Agentic%20Coding%20Trends%20Report.pdf
Why selected: This report is directly focused on agentic coding trends and presents structured analysis, case studies, and forward-looking recommendations — matching our brief and target document type. It provides a strong structural exemplar for section ordering, evidence density, and use of case examples.

Top-level sections (inferred from PDF metadata and summary reviews):
- Executive summary: high-level findings and 3-5 headline trends
- Background and definitions: what agentic coding is, terms (vibe coding, agentic workflows, MCP)
- Market adoption patterns: how teams used agentic coding in 2025 and adoption signals for 2026
- Product and platform landscape: vendor cases, tools, and platform capabilities
- Economics and cost considerations: vendor pricing models, compute, and tooling costs (likely vendor-biased)
- Case studies: examples from companies using agentic coding in production
- Risks and governance: security, trust, and safety implications
- Recommendations and next steps: what teams and vendors should do

Section purposes and evidence patterns:
- Executive summary: concise claims, bullet findings, and topline metrics (useful as a prototype for our own summary)
- Background: definitions and taxonomy; evidence via citations and simple diagrams
- Market adoption: usage stats, survey results, qualitative interviews — evidence is a mix of quantitative (surveys) and qualitative (case studies)
- Product landscape: vendor feature lists, model capabilities, integrations — evidence via vendor docs and examples
- Economics: pricing snapshots, unit-cost calculations — needs cross-vendor corroboration to avoid bias
- Case studies: narrative evidence with outcomes, ROI, and failure modes
- Risks: security incident examples, mitigations and governance structures
- Recommendations: prescriptive steps for engineering and leadership

Tone, audience, and depth notes:
- Tone: analyst/vendor hybrid; readable but intended for technical decision-makers and product leaders
- Audience: engineering leaders, platform teams, product managers, vendor strategists
- Depth: mid-to-high; includes practical examples and recommended actions rather than purely academic treatment

What to emulate structurally and what not to copy:
- Emulate: strong executive summary, taxonomy of agentic coding components, product landscape mapping, case study format, and a cost component table
- Do not copy: vendor-specific promotional passages or proprietary evaluative claims without independent verification

Table opportunities:
- A compact table comparing top agentic-coding platforms by components (model access, IDE integration, tool calls, container sessions, pricing URL)
- A per-platform cost breakdown table with direct links to pricing pages (required by special instructions)
- A features vs. capabilities matrix showing which platforms support editor control, browser/terminal control, plug-ins, and orchestration

Conclusion: Selected as the primary exemplar for structure and section purpose. We will combine Anthropic's report structure with vendor pricing pages (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Cursor, GitHub, Replit) and independent industry analyses (Deloitte, The New Stack) to produce a balanced document.
